Food Feed

To complete this project, I enlisted the help of a good friend of mine who has a lot of iOS development experience, and another friend that works as a UI/UX designer. We think our combined strengths can make a very intuitive and simple product, and hope our app will gain popularity through this.

Goals

I took on this project to improve my Android development skills, and to get a preview of what iOS development is like. We're aiming to release the app in early december, and we're hoping the app gains some traction online once published.

Challenges

Working in a team requires a lot of coordination and communication, which is difficult when faced with busy schedules and other obligations. Despite this challenge, we're working well and making sure everyone's opinion is respected and considered. The small team size has definitely helped maintain this process.

With food feed, I want to learn from the mistakes I made with my last android app . Because of this, I've been working carefully to make sure I dont fall into the same bad habits. The biggest challenge so far has been keeping my code hack-free and completely granular, since I need to learn a lot of conventions in Android to make sure I pull this off properly.

Lessons

The project isn't yet complete, but I've definitely learned a lot already through it already. Maintaining a good codebase is a lot easier than refactoring a bad one. Also, a designer is incredibly valuable.
